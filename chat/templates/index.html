Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions we receive about rehabilitation and physical therapy.

My back hurts and I can't stand. What exercises can help?

If you're experiencing back pain and difficulty standing, gentle exercises can help. Consider starting with:

  • Pelvic Tilts: Lie on your back with your knees bent. Tighten your abdominal muscles and push your lower back into the floor. Hold for 5 seconds and repeat 10 times.
  • Cat-Cow Stretch: Get on your hands and knees, and alternate between arching your back (cow) and rounding it (cat) for 10-15 repetitions.
  • Knee-to-Chest Stretch: While lying on your back, pull one knee toward your chest while keeping the other leg bent or flat on the floor. Hold for 20-30 seconds on each side.

These exercises aim to improve mobility, relieve pressure, and strengthen muscles around your spine. If pain persists, consult a healthcare professional.

What should I do if my knee hurts after a long walk?

Knee pain after walking can often be alleviated by resting, applying ice, and performing strengthening exercises. Some helpful exercises include:

  • Quad Sets: Sit with your leg straight and tighten your thigh muscle, pressing the back of your knee down into the floor. Hold for 5-10 seconds, then relax. Repeat 10-15 times.
  • Straight Leg Raises: Lie down with one leg bent and the other straight. Slowly lift your straight leg about 6 inches off the ground, hold for 5 seconds, then lower it. Do 10 repetitions on each side.
  • Hamstring Stretch: Sit on the floor with one leg extended straight and the other leg bent. Reach towards your toes of the straight leg while keeping your back straight.

If discomfort persists, it's important to consult with a physical therapist or doctor to identify the cause.

How can I reduce joint pain from arthritis?

Managing joint pain from arthritis can be approached through a combination of exercise, medication, and lifestyle changes:

  • Low-Impact Aerobics: Activities like swimming, walking, or cycling can help improve joint function without putting excess stress on the joints.
  • Strengthening Exercises: Focus on strengthening the muscles around the affected joint to reduce the load. Exercises like squats, leg raises, and light resistance training can be helpful.
  • Stretching: Gentle stretches such as the seated hamstring stretch or chest-opening stretches can help keep the joints flexible and reduce stiffness.

It's important to stay active within your comfort zone and consult your healthcare provider before starting any new exercise program.

Can physical therapy help with my posture?

Yes, physical therapy can significantly improve your posture. Through exercises aimed at strengthening your core and back muscles, physical therapy helps you maintain proper alignment and reduce muscle strain. Some key exercises include:

  • Planks: Engage your core by holding a plank position for 20-30 seconds, ensuring your body remains straight from head to heels.
  • Bridges: Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Lift your hips towards the ceiling, engaging your glutes and core muscles. Hold for 5 seconds, then lower.
  • Chin Tucks: Sit or stand up straight. Tuck your chin in towards your chest, aiming to create a double chin. Hold for 5 seconds, then relax.

These exercises help improve alignment, stability, and overall posture. Regular practice can help prevent future back and neck pain.

How long does it take to recover from physical therapy?

The duration of recovery depends on various factors, including the severity of the condition, your age, and how consistent you are with your therapy sessions and exercises. On average, patients see noticeable improvements within 4 to 6 weeks. However, full recovery can take several months depending on the complexity of the issue.

Your physical therapist will work with you to develop a tailored treatment plan and adjust it as needed based on your progress.
